## Overview

Wenqi Fan is a researcher at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University and one of the corresponding authors of the comprehensive WebAgents survey (2025). His research spans multiple areas including large foundation models, recommender systems, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and adversarial AI security.

## Research Themes

### Large Foundation Models
- Integration of LFMs across domains (web agents, protein science, healthcare)
- Emergent capabilities from billion-parameter models
- Human-like reasoning and instruction-following

### Retrieval-Augmented Generation
- Meeting LLMs with RAG architectures
- Trustworthy RAG for LLMs
- Knowledge Graph integration for recommendations

### Adversarial AI & Security
- **Black-box attacks**: Recommendation systems, social networks
- **Cross-domain attacks**: Transferable user profile copying
- **Multi-agent attacks**: Coordinated adversarial strategies
- **LLM-empowered attacks**: CheatAgent for misleading recommender systems

### Web Automation
- WebAgent architectures (perception, planning, execution)
- Training strategies for web agents
- Trustworthiness (safety, privacy, generalizability)

## Key Insights from Work

1. **RAG-LLM Integration**: Retrieval-augmented generation significantly enhances LLM capabilities for knowledge-intensive tasks
2. **Cross-domain Vulnerabilities**: Black-box recommender systems vulnerable to cross-domain profile copying attacks
3. **Multi-agent Coordination**: Coordinated multi-agent attacks more effective than single-agent approaches
4. **Trustworthiness Imperative**: Safety, privacy, and generalizability must be addressed for real-world AI deployment
5. **Knowledge Graph Value**: KG-RAG improves LLM-based recommendation quality
