## Publications

### Key Papers

**[[robots-txt-gatekeeping-paper|Is Misinformation More Open? A Study of robots.txt Gatekeeping on the Web]]** (2026)
- Published at [[www-2026|WWW '26]]
- Co-authors: [[devashish-gosain|Devashish Gosain]], [[ha-dao|Ha Dao]]
- First author
- First longitudinal study of robots.txt gatekeeping differences between reputable and misinformation websites
- Demonstrates stark disparity: 60% of reputable sites block AI crawlers vs. 9.1% of misinformation sites
- Dataset publicly available: https://doi.org/10.17617/3.WW04D8

## Contributions

### robots.txt Gatekeeping Research

**Key Findings**:
- Identified growing asymmetry in content accessibility for AI training
- Demonstrated 6.6x difference in blocking behavior between reputable and misinformation sites
- Tracked evolution from September 2023 to May 2025 showing widening gap
- Analyzed 70,410 robots.txt files across multiple snapshots
- Curated comprehensive list of 63 AI user agents

**Methodology Contributions**:
- Multi-vantage point crawling (7 geographic locations)
- Longitudinal analysis using [[internet-archive|Internet Archive]] snapshots
- [[active-blocking|Active blocking]] measurement techniques
- Integration of [[media-bias-fact-check|MBFC]] credibility classifications
- Reproducible research with public dataset

**Impact**:
- First empirical evidence of misinformation accessibility bias
- Implications for LLM training data quality
- Contributes to web transparency and data ethics debates
- Informs policy discussions on AI content sourcing
