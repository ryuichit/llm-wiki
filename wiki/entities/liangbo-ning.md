# Liangbo Ning

**Affiliation**: The Hong Kong Polytechnic University  
**Role**: Lead Author - WebAgents Survey  
**Research Areas**: Web Agents, Large Foundation Models, Recommender Systems, AI Safety

## Overview

Liangbo Ning is a researcher at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, serving as the lead author of the comprehensive survey "A Survey of WebAgents: Towards Next-Generation AI Agents for Web Automation with Large Foundation Models" (2025). His research focuses on the intersection of large foundation models and autonomous agents for web automation.

## Key Contributions

### WebAgents Survey (2025)
- **Role**: First author and lead researcher
- **Contribution**: Systematic taxonomy of WebAgent architectures (perception, planning & reasoning, execution)
- **Scope**: Comprehensive review of 45+ WebAgent systems, 45 training approaches, 190 citations
- **Impact**: First survey organizing entire WebAgent landscape with focus on trustworthiness

### Research Focus Areas

1. **AI Agent Security**: CheatAgent [105] - Attacking LLM-empowered recommender systems via LLM agents
2. **Web Automation**: Systematic study of LFM-powered agents for repetitive web tasks
3. **Training Strategies**: Coverage of training-free, GUI comprehension, task-specific fine-tuning, and post-training approaches
4. **Trustworthiness**: Safety & robustness, privacy, generalizability of WebAgents

## Research Themes

### Web Agent Architectures
- **Perception**: Text-based, screenshot-based, multi-modal approaches
- **Planning & Reasoning**: Explicit vs. implicit planning, reactive vs. strategic reasoning
- **Execution**: Grounding strategies (direct vs. inferential), interaction methods

### Training Methodologies
- **Data Construction**: Pre-processing (modality/format alignment), augmentation (collection/synthesis)
- **Training Strategies**: Training-free, GUI comprehension, task-specific fine-tuning, post-training RL
- **Data Quality**: Emphasis on high-quality sampling over indiscriminate aggregation

### Trustworthy AI
- **Safety & Robustness**: Defense against adversarial attacks, prompt injection
- **Privacy**: Protection against memory extraction, environmental injection attacks
- **Generalizability**: Addressing out-of-distribution challenges, world model integration

## Key Insights from Work

1. **Three-Process Architecture**: Perception → Planning & Reasoning → Execution is fundamental to all WebAgents
2. **Multi-modal Superiority**: Combined text and visual information outperforms single modality
3. **Strategic > Reactive**: Additional exploration and context improve action generation
4. **Training Progression**: Best results from progressive training (training-free → GUI → task-specific → post-training)
5. **Memory Criticality**: Both short-term and long-term memory dramatically improve performance

## Future Research Directions (Identified)

1. **Trustworthy WebAgents**: Fairness and explainability remain under-explored
2. **Comprehensive Benchmarks**: Need for evaluation covering real-world complexities
3. **Domain-Specific Agents**: Education and healthcare applications
4. **Personalization**: RAG systems with long-term and short-term memory
