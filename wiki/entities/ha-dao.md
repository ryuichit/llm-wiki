## Contributions

### robots.txt Gatekeeping Research

**Primary Contributions**:
- Led first systematic analysis of misinformation vs. reputable site gatekeeping
- Tracked evolution of AI crawler blocking from Sep 2023 to May 2025
- Identified critical asymmetry in content accessibility for AI training
- Curated comprehensive dataset of 63 AI user agents

**Key Findings**:
- 60% of reputable sites block AI crawlers vs. 9.1% of misinformation sites
- Gap has widened dramatically: 23% → 60% for reputable sites over 20 months
- Misinformation sites remain largely passive despite AI development events
- [[active-blocking|Active blocking]] correlates with robots.txt on reputable sites but not misinformation sites

**Methodological Innovations**:
- Multi-geographic vantage point crawling (7 locations)
- Integration of [[internet-archive|Internet Archive]] for longitudinal analysis
- Combined [[media-bias-fact-check|MBFC]] classifications with technical measurements
- Active blocking detection methodology
- Public dataset release for reproducibility

**Research Impact**:
- Raises concerns about LLM training data quality
- Informs policy discussions on web transparency
- Demonstrates limitations of voluntary compliance protocols
- Contributes empirical evidence for data ethics debates
